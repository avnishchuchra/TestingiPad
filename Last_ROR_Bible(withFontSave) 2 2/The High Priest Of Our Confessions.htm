PASTOR CHRIS

THE HIGH PRIEST
OF OUR CONFESSIONS

Back in the Old Testament, the Lord said to Moses, "...take thou unto thee Aaron thy brother, and his sons with him, from among the children of Israel, that he may minister unto me in the priest's office..." (Exodus 28:1). By that directive, God instituted the priesthood, with Aaron as the first high priest. However, God wasn't satisfied with those sacrifices of animals, because true sacrifice is spiritual. In the New Testament, God expects us to offer the calves of our lips (Hosea 14:2), not bulls and calves on a physical altar. From Hebrews 3:1, we see that under the New Testament, Jesus is our High Priest, meaning He is the one ordained by God to receive the sacrifices we have to offer. Hebrews 13:15 says "By Him (Jesus) therefore, let us offer the calves of our lips, giving thanks to His Name." The expression "giving thanks to His Name" is akin to the Greek word "homologeo," which means "to make confessions to His Name." It means "to speak the same thing in consent with another." For example, when you say, "I'm more than a conqueror through Christ Jesus," or "Greater is He that is in me,' you're offering sacrifices through your High Priest! Jesus receives such confessions of faith and presents them before the Father. This is why you must take advantage of the Lord's ministry as the High Priest of your confessions and offer good sacrifices unto God everyday by declaring what His Word says about you.

"Wherefore, holy brethren, partakers of the heavenly calling, consider the Apostle and High Priest of our profession, Christ Jesus" (Hebrews 3:1).

Recognize that your confessions of faith create realities; they're the materials with which Jesus builds His temple, and you are that temple (1 Corinthians 3:16).
