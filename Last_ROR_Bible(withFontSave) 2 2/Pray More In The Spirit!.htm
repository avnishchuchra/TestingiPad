PASTOR ANITA

PRAY MORE IN THE SPIRIT!

The Spirit of God, through the Apostle Paul, juxtaposes two kinds of prayer in 1 Corinthians 14: "praying with the spirit" and "praying with the understanding." However, His call to us is that we pray more with the spirit. That's why Paul said, "I will pray with the spirit, and I will pray with the understanding also," letting us know that praying with the spirit takes preeminence over praying with the understanding. When you pray with the spirit, your understanding is unfruitful; your mind doesn't get what you're saying, because it's coming from your spirit. But when you pray with the understanding, you're praying in a language you understand, through your mind. The proper order and priority in our prayer as revealed in Scripture is to "pray with the spirit, and then pray with the understanding also." Praying with the understanding is the "also" part of the equation. So you ought to pray more with the spirit, by speaking in other tongues. If you have it in the reverse order, you'll be shortchanging yourself. Sadly, some Christians start out by praying in their understanding and interject the prayer with a little praying in the spirit. That's not the right order!

"For if I pray in an unknown tongue, my spirit prayeth, but my understanding is unfruitful. What is it then? I will pray with the spirit, and I will pray with the understanding also: I will sing with the spirit, and I will sing with the understanding also" (1 Corinthians 14:14-15).

The Spirit's admonition is for you to pray more in other tongues than in your understanding. When you pray in this manner, your life will be dominated by the Spirit and not the senses. Your response to situations will always be from the standpoint of the Spirit! So make it a conscious practice to pray more in other tongues. Let praying in the spirit take the most part of your prayer time.
