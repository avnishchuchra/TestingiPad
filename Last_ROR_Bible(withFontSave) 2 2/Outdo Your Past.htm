PASTOR CHRIS

OUTDO YOUR PAST

In life, you must never compare yourself with others; rather, run your own race and be the best of you. The Bible talks about "the race" that is set before you. Hence, it's needless to engage in any kind of unhealthy rivalry or competition with others. You're only permitted to compete with yourself and outdo your past. Never allow yourself to be discouraged when it seems as though those around you are making more progress than you are. That someone else is succeeding doesn't mean you're failing: it all depends on individual aims and targets, or set objectives. Always remember that you're not running the same race. Success is relative and is measured at different stages. For example, two men are building their houses; one lays a not too- deep foundation and starts erecting a bungalow, while the other one takes his time to dig deep because he wants to erect a five-storey building. The former may finish his building and even start living in it while the latter is yet digging his foundation. These two men aren't aiming for the same thing. The fellow who spent a long time digging knows his foundation has to be deep enough to accommodate the tall building he intends to put up, for the higher you want to build, the deeper your foundation ought to be. At the end of the day, when both houses are built, the difference will be clear between the two buildings. If the fellow building the storeyed house had gotten into unhealthy competition and became discouraged that the other fellow had already moved into his house while he was still digging his foundation, he would never have completed his own masterpiece.

"Brethren, I count not myself to have apprehended: but this one thing I do, forgetting those things which are behind, and reaching forth unto those things which are before" (Philippians 3:13).

So in life, focus on your race! Set your gaze upon the Master and run your race to win, seeking only to outdo your past.
