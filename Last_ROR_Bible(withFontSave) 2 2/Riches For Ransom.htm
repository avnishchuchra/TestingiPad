PASTOR CHRIS

RICHES FOR RANSOM

The things God has given you aren't just for you to store up and look at. They're for you to redeem your soul. They're for you to ransom your life and that of your loved ones. There are times when all your talking and quoting of scripture wouldn't be enough. At those times, only a sacrificial offering will settle the problem! Therefore, all you need do is look around your house and pull out that sacrifice and place it on the altar of God. A dear friend of mine, who is a minister of God, once shared the testimony of how his daughter got sick and wouldn't get well even after series of intense prayers. The doctors couldn't diagnose what was wrong with the little girl. Her lips became black and everyone who saw her wondered what kind of sickness she was suffering from. Every attempt at getting the child well just didn't work. Then he opened his wardrobe and packed out all his suits which were about thirty and took them to church, and gave them as an offering. A few days after giving that offering, his daughter was miraculously restored to health. Many Christians don't understand this stuff and that's why they get into all kinds of trouble. When you study Solomon's life you'll discover that the key to his success was in his giving; he was a man who distinguished himself with his offering. Not even David, his father, gave as much as he did. This was why God appeared to him after his phenomenal sacrifice and said, "Ask what you will" (1 Kings 3:2-5).

"The ransom of a man's life are his riches: but the poor heareth not rebuke" (Proverbs 13:8).

His giving endeared him to the heart of God.
