PASTOR ANITA

ARE YOU A MINUS
OR A PLUS?

There are two kinds of people- minus-people and plus-people. A minus person is the kind of person you leave in an office only to come back and find the chair or some other thing broken. When you take him to your house, he just turns everything upside down; he breaks furniture and your household appliances start having faults. But a plus person treats everything good; the house is always clean when he's around and he never uses bad language. A minus person has a critical spirit. For example, he would always criticize the choir ministrations in church, but a plus person wouldn't think like that. Rather, he'll decide to join the choir and add fresh ideas to what they're doing. Always, he's thinking about increase. He's the kind of fellow who is always looking out for someone to help. He's always doing something new and different for people, and the Kingdom of God is always uppermost on his mind. That's unlike the minus person who's ever in a hurry and wouldn't see the need around him. The man that's a plus is the man with an excellent spirit. The Bible described Daniel as a man with an excellent spirit (Daniel 6:3). Daniel became a leader of leaders. When you study his life, you'll observe that many others plotted against him but they failed. A man that's a plus will always excel above his colleagues, and he would certainly have critics. But minus people are spiritually myopic! If you're a Christian then you're a plus in your home and in your office; you're a plus wherever you go.

"Then this Daniel was preferred above the presidents and princes, because an excellent spirit was in him; and the king thought to set him over the whole realm" (Daniel 6:3).

Why? Because God has increased you and you're not working with your ability, you're working with His ability. Therefore, men will see you excel and give glory to God (Romans 8:19).
