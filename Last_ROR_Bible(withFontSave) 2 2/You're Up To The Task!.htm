PASTOR CHRIS

YOU'RE UP TO THE TASK!

The word "sufficiency" in 2 Corinthians 3:5 also means "competence." It is translated from the Greek word "hikanotes" which means to have competence or ability. It refers to the quality of being adequately qualified physically, intellectually and in other areas for the purpose of carrying out a task. Put simply, it means to be up to the task. The Apostle Paul in (2 Corinthians 3:5) was saying we're up to the task, any task! That means no matter the task or assignment committed to you, see yourself as the right man or woman for the job! If you would imbibe this mindset, it'll set you apart for greatness and success, be it in your work, at school, or in your business. Prior to this time, you may have been described as that seemingly incompetent person-the guy who always messes things up, but God is saying to you today, "Son, daughter, you're up to the task! You have super-competence, for your sufficiency is of Me!" You're competent enough to face whatever challenges come your way and produce excellence. You're up to that task in the office! Refuse to believe those who consider you not good enough for the job; tell yourself, "I am up to the task." Remember, your "competence" is not described by your own physical or mental prowess, but according to the supernatural ability of God working in you. This was what gave the Apostle Paul the "I-can-domentality" (Philippians 4:13)! Like Paul, recognize today that God is at work in you and you function with His ability.

"Not that we are sufficient of ourselves to think any thing as of ourselves; but our sufficiency is of God;" (2 Corinthians 3:5).

Never act as though you're not qualified for any task, even if it's a new assignment on your job. Remember, the Spirit of God is your sufficiency; He has made you competent-you're up to any task.
