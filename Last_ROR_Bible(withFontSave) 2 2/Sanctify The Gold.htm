PASTOR CHRIS

SANCTIFY THE GOLD

In Matthew 23:16-17, the Lord Jesus shows us the relationship between the temple and the gold. "The temple sanctifies the gold." We're 'the temple' of God (1 Corinthians 3:16) and 'the gold' in today's terms would refer to money. This means that as God's New Testament temple, your responsibility is to sanctify (that is, dedicate to God) the money that comes to you. "How do I do that?" you might want to ask. I'll show you from the scriptures. The children of Israel had been slaves in Egypt for hundreds of years. But after God wrought mighty miracles in the land through Moses, the Egyptians gave them all their gold, silver, clothes, etc, and begged them to leave. As the children of Israel journeyed through the wilderness, God told them to use all the treasures they gathered from Egypt to build a sanctuary for Him (Exodus 25:1-8). God caused the wealth of Egypt to be transferred into the hands of His people. His people in turn used this wealth to build a sanctuary for Him. In the same way today, God puts His children in positions where money can get into their hands to use in accomplishing His plans and purposes on earth. So you see, the reason you're in that job or business is to sanctify the money that comes to you.

"Woe unto you, ye blind guides, which say, Whosoever shall swear by the temple, it is nothing; but whosoever shall swear by the gold of the temple, he is a debtor! Ye fools and blind: for whether is greater, the gold, or the temple that sanctifieth the gold?" (Matthew 23:16-17).

Each time you put money into the things of God, the work of the ministry progresses, more souls are won to Christ and the Church expands throughout the world. That way, you're helping to build a habitation of God through the Spirit. God on His part ensures bigger doors of blessings are opened for you to function more effectively in His Kingdom and in the affairs of men.
