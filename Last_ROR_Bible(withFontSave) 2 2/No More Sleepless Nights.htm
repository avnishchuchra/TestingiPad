PASTOR ANITA

NO MORE SLEEPLESS NIGHTS

I believe one of the greatest things in life is to know that you're loved by God, the Creator of the universe. The Bible says He cares for you affectionately and cares about you watchfully. This world is full of troubles and you will face different challenges as long as you're living in it. But the good news is that Jesus overcame the world for your sake (John 16:33). Therefore, you should no longer have sleepless nights over any situation in your life. Some people find it difficult to sleep when they're confronted with challenges. Such people are oblivious of the fact that they were called into a life of rest. The Bible says, "For he that is entered into his rest, he also hath ceased from his own works, as God did from his" (Hebrews 4:10). Jesus was mocked to the cross; He was spat on, beaten, rejected, and then He died. All these He did so that you may no longer cry. Therefore, whenever you think about the death of Jesus, always remember that He took all those pains, worries, and dejection which could have caused you the sleepless nights. Remember that when He was praying in the garden of Gethsemane just before He was arrested, His disciples were sleeping. They awoke only after He had finished praying and He said to them, "Sleep on now, and take your rest..." (Matthew 26:45). The Bible says, "For he that is entered into his rest, he also hath ceased from his own works, as God did from his" (Hebrews 4:10).

"Casting all your care upon him; for he careth for you" (1 Peter 5:7).

He has given you rest and taken your sleepless nights away. 1 Peter 5:7 tells you what to do when you're faced with challenges. Therefore, never let worry or any other thing steal your rest away from you.
