PASTOR CHRIS

YOU HAVE "STAYING POWER"

As a child of God, patience is one quality that is inherent in your recreated human spirit. It's the ability to be unswerving or undeterred from your deliberate purpose and faith, even by the greatest of trials. I like to term it "staying power" or "stickability." This is akin to the Greek word "hupomone," which means patience or endurance. It's the ability to stick with something to the very end, despite staggering oppositions; the ability to hold on to what you believe despite the pressure that the devil, your circumstances or even your mind may be piling against you. Some people give up at the slightest sign of trouble, but if you're born again, you have to learn to stick and endure situations that try your faith. The good news is God has deposited this quality in you. In 2 Timothy 2:3, we're asked to endure hardness as good soldiers of Christ. If it wasn't in you to endure, God would be remiss to ask you to do so. You have to learn to keep keeping on, despite the trials you face. Some people fail in life because they give up too soon; they haven't learnt to stick with a situation or process until they win. The challenges you face are the stepping-stones to your promotion; they can't destroy you. Therefore, don't pack-up or give up when challenges show up in your life. God has given you the strength and courage to endure every contrary situation. You might be facing some "heat" at work, school, or in your home, but remember this: you have the ability to face any challenge and win.

"Knowing this, that the trying of your faith worketh patience" (James 1:3).

Learn to put the patience and staying power that's in you to work. Let your endurance be mixed with hope so you can create a better future right in the midst of the storm.
