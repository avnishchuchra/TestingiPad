PASTOR CHRIS


THE PREVAILING POWER OF PRAISE

In quoting part of a Psalm of David, Jesus said in Matthew 21:16, "have ye never read, Out of the mouth of babes and sucklings thou hast perfected praise?" But the Psalmist actually said, "out of the mouth of babes thou has ordained strength." In other words, Jesus connects praise with strength. There's such an awesome power that's released when you begin to praise God. If you want to continuously see the power and glory of God in your life, you must learn to praise God lavishly. I'm not just talking about praising Him when everything is going smoothly with your job, family, business, ministry, academics or finances. I'm talking about maintaining a lifestyle of praise and thanksgiving unto God, irrespective and independent of circumstances. Such praise creates a force-field of God's power in and around you, causing circumstances, devils, sicknesses, diseases and everything that's not consistent with the will of God to be completely repelled from your life. The Bible says the joy of the Lord is your strength (Nehemiah 8:10). That's what the Psalmist is saying. Strength there refers to power; and that power is what stills the enemy and the avenger. When you praise God by openly declaring His majesty, the works of the enemy are completely exterminated from your life. One of the Hebrew synonyms for the phrase "still the enemy" as used in Psalm 8:2 is "to destroy the enemy" or "to paralyze the enemy."

"Out of the mouth of babes and sucklings hast thou ordained strength because of thine enemies, that thou mightest still the enemy and the avenger" (Psalm 8:2).

This means that the praise of God that proceeds out of your mouth is a deathblow to the enemy. Never allow the praise of God depart from your lips. It creates a forcefield of God's power that repels the enemy.
