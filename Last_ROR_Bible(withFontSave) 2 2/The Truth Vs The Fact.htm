THE TRUTH VS THE FACT

Someone once defined 'fact' as a piece of information about circumstances that exist or events that have occurred. However, the 'truth' is what the Word of God says about the same circumstances or events. The Word is truth (John 17:17); it is reality. Job 22:29 clearly shows the difference between the fact and the truth. It shows us that even though it's a fact that everything is hopeless and there's no sign that things are going to get better, we can still declare the truth of God and say, "We're lifted up everywhere." Hallelujah! It's very important that you realize the difference between the fact and the truth. Otherwise, you wouldn't know how to take yourself out of any undesirable situation using the principle of confession. For example, a man had an accident and the x-ray showed that his bone was broken. When he was told, he said, "No, the bone is not broken in Jesus' Name!" That was wrong! There's a difference between denying the fact and denying fact the power to determine the circumstances of your existence. The Bible never told us to deny the fact of our situation; rather, it says we should proclaim, confess or affirm the truth of God's Word in the situation. It was a fact that the man's bones were broken, however, the truth says, "...by whose (Jesus') stripes ye were healed" (1 Peter 2:24). So, the man's declaration ought to have been: "By the stripes of Jesus Christ I am healed." Declaration of the truth activates the power of God causing the situation to conform to your declaration.

" When men are cast down, then thou shalt say, There is lifting up; and he shall save the humble person" (Job 22:29).

Therefore, refuse to be dominated by the facts of your situation, rather let the truth of God be your focus. Fill your ears, eyes and mouth with it, so you can effect any change you desire in your life today.
