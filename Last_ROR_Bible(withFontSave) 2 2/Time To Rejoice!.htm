PASTOR ANITA

TIME TO REJOICE

The Word of God leaves us in no doubt as to the way we ought to live as Christians. We ought to live a happy and joyous life always. You ought to 'rejoice' 'in the Lord, no matter what happens around you. I want you to notice the double emphasis on the word "rejoice" in Philippians 4:4. It lets us know that it's your season to rejoice! It's the set time for your joy to be full. You might want to ask, "What if the circumstances of my life aren't such that should give room for rejoicing?" Well, rejoice all the same! Refuse to allow the circumstances of life dictate your joy. You have to understand that your happy spirit dictates what goes on around you. I'm talking about being joyful in the Spirit-the kind of joy that transcends physical circumstances and emotions. When your faith is challenged, that's the time to rejoice. The Bible says that with joy you shall draw water from the wells of salvation (Isaiah 12:3). Salvation is an allencompassing word; it refers to soundness, preservation, divine health, success, prosperity and every blessing that the death, burial and resurrection of Jesus Christ made available for the Christian. In other words, God wants you to know that you must keep your joy afloat in order to experience the wonderful blessings of salvation. Learn to maintain a joyful spirit even in the midst of daunting and trying circumstances, for the joy of the Lord is your strength (Nehemiah8:10).

"Rejoice in the Lord alway: and again I say, Rejoice" (Philippians 4:4).

Rejoicing in the face ofadversity demonstrates that your faith is strong and you're living in the finished works of Christ. Understand that sin, sickness, poverty, failure and the devil have all been defeated; so nothing can successfully stand against you!
