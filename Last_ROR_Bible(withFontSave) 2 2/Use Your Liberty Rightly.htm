PASTOR CHRIS

USE YOUR LIBERTY RIGHTLY

When you were born again, you were born into a life of absolute liberty in Christ. You became free to live, free to serve God, free to reign and free to love. However, as Christians, the Bible admonishes that we use our liberty in Christ rightly. Under the Old Testament, it was easy to read the commandments so as to know what to do and what not to do, but the new creation man wasn't given any law to obey (1 Timothy 1:9). According to the Bible, it's difficult to impute sin where there is no law (Romans 4:15). Some Christians are in a dilemma because they have been brought into a life without laws as it seems, and so they use that as an excuse to act uncircumspectly. The life you live as a child of God should be to His praise and glory in all things. Though it is absolutely clear that God has called you to a free life, only make sure that you don't use this freedom as an excuse to do whatever you want to do and as a result, destroy your freedom. Rather, use your freedom to serve others in love; that's how freedom grows. Use your liberty in Christ rightly by doing only those things that please the Father; and the way to please the Father is walking by faith, which by extension is walking according to the provisions of God's Word. Yes, God isn't imposing rules and regulations on you, but make up your mind to get into His Word and do what the Word says. It's a choice you have to make.

"For, brethren, ye have been called unto liberty; only use not liberty for an occasion to the flesh, but by love serve one another" (Galatians 5:13).

God has brought us into a life of absolute liberty and He'll never force anything on us, but be sure to use your liberty to the praise and glory of His Name.
